Emotional Intelligence

c2 emotional intelligence post

What learnings did you take from the chapter?

"Happiness is a skill that can be trained", and that happiness can be tied to an exceptionally healthy mind, or an optimal state of being - and an unavoidable side effect of emotional intelligence.

I also found it interesting that of the top six competencies that separate star performers from average performers in the tech sector, only two were based on intellectual abilities. Even more surprising, the top two were influenced on emotional intelligence.

Watching the short video, I also found Chade-Meng Tan's talk on the mindset shift when exercising mindfulness, from existential to experiential as your perception into the process of emotion sharpens. Once you become more aware of your emotions, you begin to view your emotions in a Big Sky Mindset - which is being able to identify when you are experiencing certain emotions instead of going "I'm angry" or "I'm sad" to "I'm experiencing anger" or "I'm experiencing sadness".

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?

I found Search Inside Yourself - The unexpected path to happiness very interesting, and will finish reading the book in my spare time.

I would also like to practice and add some of his suggested exercises to help increase my mindfulness and happiness into my daily routine.

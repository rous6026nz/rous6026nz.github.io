Time & Habits

Which timebox and habits techniques did I try?

In the past, I have used a similar strategy similar to the Pomodoro technique, where I would identify my todo tasks, allocate time to each task, then begin work on each task stopping every 30 minutes for a 5 minute break to get a drink of water, go toilet or walk around the building.

The Pomodoro technique seems more structured, with accurate time keeping. It also helps with accurately defining timelines for each task, and breaking each task into sizable chunks of work.

What worked for me?

The Pomodoro technique is something which I feel will work will for me when tackling projects. I will also like to try meditation, I have never try meditation, but can image how it could help with clarative thinking and relieving stress levels.

In the past, when I came up against a blocker when programming, I would leave the computer and do something totally different - like go to the beach, and 9 times out of 10, I would find a solution either while I'm doing something else or when re-visiting the issue. It felt like although I wasn't thinking about the issue, my brain was subconsciously trying to solve the problem.

Which will I experiment further with?

I want to experiment with meditation, both focal and mindful. I was unknowingly performing meditation in the past while admiring the view from my desktop at work as well as while working through the bush walk next to my office.

I also want to experiement with the Pomodoro timeboxing technique as well.

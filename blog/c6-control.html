When programming, what are some of your triggers?

Something that always tends to trigger me when programming is working on code that is untidy, has been badly maintained or worked on by other developers with bad coding habits and follow no consistent stanards or conventions.

When developers write code that uses inconsistent naming conventions for variables and functions, the lack of commenting and spacing or incorrectly indented code makes it harder to read and scan.

What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

Untidy code or programmers with a lack of standards or pride in their work is probably what causes the most emotional response for me. Working on untidy code is hard to read, follow, understand and can cause extra work for any developer working on the code in the future.

It's annoying having to go through and tidy up another developers poor workmanship prior to starting any work, or even worst having to follow those conventions.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise?

While reading about the SBNRR process, I kept reflecting back to two situations I experienced recently at mahi. In one of the situations I was triggered and once that happened my mind was stuck on what the person said which started the trigger. And in return found it hard to focus on the rest of the conversation and giving the speaker my full attention.

After the conversation, we repeated back to each other our interpretation of the conversation and because my attention was stuck on the trigger, my interpretation of the conversation was not complete and I missed some key points.

During the readings about SBNRR, I was able to relate the readings back to these experiences and that helped me better understand the feelings and emotions experienced and the concepts being explained in the readings.

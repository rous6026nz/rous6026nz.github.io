What is a responsive site?

A responsive website is a website that has been optimized to be viewed on various devices and screen sizes, automatically resizing and reordering its content to suit the users browsing experience.

Why is responsiveness important?

Having a website that is responsive and optimized for small screens as well as desktops is important for a number of reasons - it can have an impact on your websites SEO ranking with Google and because of the rapid growth in smartphone usage, provide smartphone users with a much better user experience.

What is mobile first design?

Mobile first design is a term used when a designer begins to design a website starting with the mobile screen size first. When designing a website with this approach it helps to identify and ensure the most important information to user is clear and easy to access and read, provides a simplified version of the design to work up from and allows for progressive enhancements as more screen realestate becomes available.

Why is mobile first design important?

With the rapid growth of the smartphone and tablet markets of the past 5 - 10 years, and double digit growth of mobile internet usage, not having a mobile optimized website will provide an unpleasant and frustrating experience for the end user, potentially losing you a potential client, or if your an eCommerce retailer, a customer and have a negitive impact on your conversion rate.

What are frameworks?

A framework is a library of predefined reusable methods, components and/or classes which can be used to build functional applications quickly. Frameworks can also be used as a base for building your application on and can be extended on.

What are the pros and cons of using frameworks?

The advantages of using a framework is being able to build complex applications with minimal amount of code and time, having access to a library of built-in methods, components and utility classes and the developer community support.

The disadvantages to using a framework is the potential learning curve, in the early stages of getting used to and using a framework is knowing what, where and how you can extend the framework without breaking it.

What is a wireframes?

Wireframes are rough sketches/designs outlining a webpages structure and elements. Wireframes are can be hand-drawn or computer rendered designs, and usually contain boxes, lines and crosses inside a box to depict a container, a line of text or an image element.

Why use wireframes?

Wireframes are usually the first step in the design process and are often used to define the core structure of a webpage. For the developer it is useful when building the basic structure of the webpage and helps gain an understanding of what elements are required.

What aspects of my wireframes I found difficult to implement, and why?

The parts of my wireframe designs I had the most difficulties implementing were the overlapping elements, these were difficult to position right and ensuring they scaled correctly when the screen resized.
